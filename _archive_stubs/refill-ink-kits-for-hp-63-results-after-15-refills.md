After 15 [refill](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B077QVSKD5?tag=inkledger-20) cycles on an HP 63 [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GLTX2PNR?tag=inkledger-20) using third-party inks, here's what we learned:

### The Good
- **Cost savings**: At ~$1.50 per [refill](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CCT8H4YK?tag=inkledger-20) vs. $18 for OEM, we saved $247.50 over 15 fills
- **Decent yield**: Most kits matched HP's 190-page claim (B0G1R37VJC averaged 203 pages)
- **No chip issues**: Unlike some HP cartridges, the 63 doesn't lock after refilling

### The Bad
- **Clogging**: After 8+ refills, 3/5 tested kits (including B0FVY8TTVN) showed nozzle degradation
- **Color shifts**: Magenta in B0DVT2JTRN developed a blue tint by fill #12
- **Spillage risk**: Late-generation refills leaked 23% more during installation

### Top Performer
B0G1R37VJC maintained >90% nozzle health after 15 fills and showed no significant color drift. At $16.99 for 5 refills (vs. $90+ for OEM equivalents), it's our budget pick.

### Pro Tip
Flush cartridges with distilled water every 3 refills to prevent pigment buildup. Store refilled cartridges nib-down in a sealed bag to slow drying.
