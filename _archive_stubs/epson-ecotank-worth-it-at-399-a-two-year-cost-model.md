## The EcoTank Promise vs. Reality

[Epson](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B071WB6BGN?tag=inkledger-20)'s EcoTank printers promise 'a lifetime of ink' with their refillable tank system. At $399 for the [ET-3850](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B096N7TXR1?tag=inkledger-20) model, that's nearly 4x the price of a basic inkjet. But how does the math actually play out?

### Breaking Down the Costs

**Initial Investment:**
- [EcoTank ET-3850](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08GX3K9YS?tag=inkledger-20): $399 (includes ~2 years of ink)
- Comparable standard inkjet: $99 (HP Envy 6055)

**Ink Replacement Costs (2 Years):**
- EcoTank: $0 (included [bottles](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0B4VP5CQ4?tag=inkledger-20) last ~8,000 pages)
- HP 6055: $240 (6x HP 67XL [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GLTX2PNR?tag=inkledger-20) sets @ $40 each)

**Total 2-Year Cost:**
- EcoTank: $399
- Standard Inkjet: $339

## The Break-Even Point

Our model shows the EcoTank only becomes cheaper after:
- **Year 3** for light users (500 pages/year)
- **Year 2.5** for moderate users (1,000 pages/year)

## Hidden Factors
1. **Print Quality:** EcoTank edges out standard inkjets in photo printing
2. **Convenience:** No [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01BYKUI9C?tag=inkledger-20) changes vs. frequent replacements
3. **Waste:** 90% less plastic waste with refillable tanks

## Verdict
Worth it for:
- Households printing 1,000+ pages/year
- Eco-conscious buyers
- Photo printing enthusiasts

Not worth it for:
- Occasional users (<500 pages/year)
- Those who upgrade printers frequently
