When setting up a home office, choosing the right [printer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B077QVSKD5?tag=inkledger-20) can have a significant impact on your long-term ink costs. [Canon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B079Y6FHKJ?tag=inkledger-20) offers two popular lines of inkjet printers for home office use: the MAXIFY series, designed for high-volume printing, and the PIXMA series, which caters to general home use. Here’s how they compare in terms of ink costs and efficiency.

### Canon MAXIFY: Built for Volume

The **MAXIFY** series (like the [MAXIFY GX7021](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0G1R37VJC?tag=inkledger-20)) is engineered for small offices or home users with heavy printing needs. Key features:
- **High-yield cartridges**: MAXIFY uses larger ink tanks (e.g., PGI-2800XXL) that last longer, reducing the frequency of replacements.
- **Ink efficiency**: Canon claims MAXIFY printers use up to **2x less ink** per page compared to PIXMA models for text-heavy documents.
- **Cost per page**: Approximately **$0.03–$0.05** for [black](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0FKBFH92Q?tag=inkledger-20)-and-white pages, depending on [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GLTX2PNR?tag=inkledger-20) prices.

### Canon PIXMA: Affordable for Light Use

The **PIXMA** series (e.g., [PIXMA TR4720](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0FVY8TTVN?tag=inkledger-20)) is more budget-friendly upfront but may cost more over time:
- **Standard cartridges**: Uses smaller cartridges (e.g., PG-245/CL-246) that need frequent replacements.
- **Cost per page**: Around **$0.08–$0.12** for [black](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B081GRVJ5K?tag=inkledger-20)-and-white, nearly double the MAXIFY’s rate.
- **[Refill](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CCT8H4YK?tag=inkledger-20) options**: Third-party [compatible](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CQ4VHBMH?tag=inkledger-20) cartridges (like [B0DVT2JTRN](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DVT2JTRN?tag=inkledger-20)) can cut costs by 50% but may void warranties.

### Which Should You Choose?
- **High-volume users**: MAXIFY saves money long-term despite higher upfront costs.
- **Light/moderate users**: PIXMA is cheaper initially but may cost more in ink over years.

**Pro Tip**: Check Canon’s [Instant Ink](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DQQ77WYT?tag=inkledger-20) subscription—it can lower costs for both lines if you print regularly.

For refillable options, consider [ink tank models](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DPHQRLJC?tag=inkledger-20) to avoid [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01BYKUI9C?tag=inkledger-20) markup entirely.
