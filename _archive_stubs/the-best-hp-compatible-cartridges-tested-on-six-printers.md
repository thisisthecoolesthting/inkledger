## Why compatible cartridges matter

HP's Instant Ink program and OEM [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GLTX2PNR?tag=inkledger-20) pricing turns your [printer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B077QVSKD5?tag=inkledger-20) into a subscription service. Compatible cartridges from third-party manufacturers can cut costs by 60% or more — but only if they actually work.

We ran 12 third-party HP-compatible cartridges through six popular HP printers ([OfficeJet](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CFM82NS2?tag=inkledger-20) Pro [9015e](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CT8ZJ7ZC?tag=inkledger-20), Envy 6055, [LaserJet](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B09N4Z8DNL?tag=inkledger-20) Pro M15w, and others) with brutal real-world tests:

- **Yield tests**: Printed until dry with 5% page coverage
- **Quality checks**: Scanned 1200dpi samples of text/graphics/photo prints
- **Reliability**: Installed/uninstalled cartridges 20x to test chip recognition

## When to stick with OEM

- Printing legal/medical documents
- Using HP Instant Ink subscription
- Warranty concerns (some void coverage for third-party ink)

For everyone else? These tested compatibles deliver the same page at half the cost.
