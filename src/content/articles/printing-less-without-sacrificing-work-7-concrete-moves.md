### 1. Default to ‘Draft Mode’ for Internal Documents

Most printers hide an ink-saving superpower: draft mode reduces ink coverage by up to 50% while keeping text legible. For school assignments or meeting agendas, this setting cuts costs immediately. (Pro tip: Pair with [high-yield compatible cartridges](#) for maximum savings.)

### 2. The 24-Hour Print Rule

Implement a waiting period before hitting print. If a document isn’t urgently needed, save it as a PDF and revisit the next day. Studies show 30% of home prints get discarded within hours—this habit alone could save a cartridge every few months.

### 3. Two-Font Rule

Stick to two efficient fonts: a sans-serif like Arial for body text (uses 27% less ink than Times New Roman) and one accent font. Avoid heavy bold/italic overuse, which increases ink consumption.

### 4. The Margin Hack

Reduce default margins from 1” to 0.75” in Word or Google Docs. This fits 15% more content per page without compromising readability. For multi-page documents, the savings add up fast.

### 5. Smart Scanning Replacements

Instead of printing contracts or school permission slips:
- Use your phone’s document scanner (built into iOS Notes or Android Files)
- Sign digitally with free tools like [SimpleSign](#)
- Email or cloud-share the final version

### 6. Printer Maintenance Matters

A clogged printhead wastes ink on failed prints. Run a nozzle check monthly if you print infrequently. For laser users, redistribute toner by gently shaking cartridges when prints get faint.

### 7. The Paper Tray Trick

Keep only 10 sheets in your printer’s tray. Physical limitation creates mindful printing—when you have to reload paper, you’ll reconsider if the print is truly necessary.

*Bonus for parents:* Set printers to grayscale by default. Kids’ homework rarely needs color, and this prevents accidental color cartridge drain.
