## HP 910 vs HP 910XL: Which Cartridge is the Better Value?

Choosing the right ink cartridge for your HP printer can be confusing. The HP 910 and HP 910XL are two common options, but understanding their differences is crucial to getting the most for your money. At first glance, the "XL" version seems like the obvious choice. But is it *always* the best value? Let's break down the page yield, cost, and cost-per-page to determine which cartridge truly wins in the long run. We'll also examine compatible alternatives that can significantly reduce your printing expenses.

## Understanding Page Yield and Cartridge Capacity

The primary difference between the HP 910 and HP 910XL cartridges is their page yield – the estimated number of pages you can print with a single cartridge. HP provides these estimates based on industry-standard testing, but real-world results can vary depending on your printing habits.

*   **HP 910 (Standard Yield):** Typically yields around 300 pages for the black cartridge and 315 pages for each color cartridge (cyan, magenta, yellow).
*   **HP 910XL (High Yield):** Offers a significantly higher yield, with approximately 825 pages for the black cartridge and 825 pages for each color cartridge.

These numbers represent ideal printing conditions (5% coverage). If you print graphics, photos, or text with heavy formatting, you'll likely see a lower page yield. Still, the XL cartridges offer a clear advantage in terms of total pages printed.

### The Fine Print on "Yield" Estimates

It's important to remember that printer manufacturers often base page yield estimates on 5% coverage, meaning that only 5% of the page is covered in ink. Most documents, especially those with images or graphics, will have a much higher coverage. This means your actual page yield will likely be lower than the advertised numbers. You can try to calibrate your inkjet to print darker without using so much ink per page, which is covered more in depth on our pillar page: [/pillars/mvp-pillar-1-calibrating-your-inkjet-to-print-darker-without-usi/].

## Cost Comparison: OEM vs. Compatible Cartridges

The initial purchase price is a significant factor when choosing between the HP 910 and HP 910XL. However, a lower upfront cost doesn't always translate to long-term savings. Let's compare the approximate prices (as of October 2024) of both OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) and compatible cartridges:

| Cartridge Type | OEM Price (Approx.) | Compatible Price (Approx.) |
| ---------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------- |
| HP 910 Black     | $25                | $12                        |
| HP 910 Color     | $20 each           | $10 each                   |
| HP 910XL Black    | $45                | $20                        |
| HP 910XL Color    | $35 each           | $18 each                   |

*Note: Prices can vary depending on the retailer and current promotions.*

As you can see, the XL cartridges are more expensive upfront. However, the real cost comparison comes down to cost per page.

### Calculating Cost Per Page (CPP)

Cost per page (CPP) is the most accurate way to determine which cartridge offers the best value. To calculate CPP, divide the cartridge price by the estimated page yield:

*   **HP 910 Black:** $25 / 300 pages = $0.08 per page
*   **HP 910 Color:** $20 / 315 pages = $0.06 per page
*   **HP 910XL Black:** $45 / 825 pages = $0.05 per page
*   **HP 910XL Color:** $35 / 825 pages = $0.04 per page

Based on these calculations, the **HP 910XL cartridges offer a lower cost per page** than the standard HP 910 cartridges. This means that, over time, you'll likely save money by purchasing the XL versions, assuming you print frequently enough to use the extra ink before it dries out (this is especially important given the information laid out in our pillar page: [/pillars/mvp-pillar-3-storing-unused-cartridges-the-vacuum-sealing-trick/]).

### The Compatible Cartridge Advantage

Now, let's factor in compatible cartridges. Using the compatible prices from the table above:

*   **Compatible HP 910 Black:** $12 / 300 pages = $0.04 per page
*   **Compatible HP 910 Color:** $10 / 315 pages = $0.03 per page
*   **Compatible HP 910XL Black:** $20 / 825 pages = $0.02 per page
*   **Compatible HP 910XL Color:** $18 / 825 pages = $0.02 per page

As you can see, compatible cartridges offer substantial savings, often cutting the cost per page in half or more. Even the standard yield compatible cartridges can be more cost-effective than OEM XL cartridges.

## Which Cartridge is Right for You?

Choosing between the HP 910 and HP 910XL (and considering compatible alternatives) depends on your printing volume and budget.

*   **Low-Volume Printing (Occasional Use):** If you only print occasionally (a few pages per week), the standard HP 910 cartridges might be sufficient. However, consider compatible versions to save money. The risk of ink drying out in a partly-used XL cartridge is higher if your printing is infrequent.
*   **Medium-Volume Printing (Regular Use):** If you print regularly (several pages per week), the HP 910XL cartridges are generally the better value due to their lower cost per page. Opting for compatible XL cartridges will maximize your savings.
*   **High-Volume Printing (Frequent Use):** For frequent printers, compatible HP 910XL cartridges are the most cost-effective option. You might also consider investing in an EcoTank or MegaTank printer (see: [/products/b012iwpsay-epson-664-ecotank-ink-ultra-high-capacity-bottle-black-t6641/]) that uses refillable ink tanks, which can significantly reduce printing costs in the long run.

### Factors to Consider

*   **Printer Model:** Ensure that the cartridges you choose are compatible with your specific HP printer model. Check your printer's manual or HP's website for compatibility information.
*   **Print Quality:** While compatible cartridges have improved significantly in recent years, some users report slight differences in print quality compared to OEM cartridges. Read reviews and consider testing a small batch before committing to a large purchase.
*   **Warranty:** While it's a common misconception that using compatible cartridges voids your printer warranty, this is generally not the case. However, always check your printer's warranty terms and conditions for specific details.
*   **Storage:** Proper storage is crucial for maintaining ink cartridge quality. Store cartridges in a cool, dark place, ideally in their original packaging, to prevent drying or damage.

## Making the Switch to Compatible Cartridges: Tips and Tricks

If you're considering switching to compatible cartridges, here are a few tips to ensure a smooth transition:

1.  **Research Reputable Brands:** Not all compatible cartridges are created equal. Look for brands with positive reviews and a solid reputation for quality and reliability.
2.  **Read Customer Reviews:** Pay attention to customer feedback regarding print quality, cartridge longevity, and any potential issues with printer compatibility.
3.  **Check for Warranty and Return Policies:** Choose suppliers that offer a warranty or return policy in case of defective cartridges or compatibility issues.
4.  **Clean Your Printhead Regularly:** To prevent clogging and ensure optimal print quality, clean your printer's printhead regularly. Refer to your printer's manual for instructions.

## Conclusion: Savings are Achievable with Informed Choices

The choice between HP 910 and HP 910XL cartridges ultimately depends on your individual printing needs and budget. While the XL cartridges offer a lower cost per page, compatible cartridges provide even greater savings. By carefully considering your printing volume, researching reputable brands, and following our tips and tricks, you can significantly reduce your printing expenses without sacrificing print quality. Don't let printer manufacturers dictate your spending – take control of your ink costs and make informed decisions that benefit your wallet.
