After testing 8 popular PG-245 refill kits against Canon's OEM cartridges, we found staggering differences in color fidelity - even between kits costing the same. Here's how they stacked up:

## Methodology
We printed standardized test pages (containing gradients, skin tones, and Pantone swatches) using:
- Fresh OEM Canon PG-245 cartridge (baseline)
- 8 refill kits from top Amazon sellers (B0G1R37VJC, B0FVY8TTVN, etc.)
- Identical Canon Pixma TR4720 printer
- 92 Brightness 24lb paper

Color accuracy was measured with an X-Rite i1Pro spectrophotometer, evaluating Delta-E values (lower = more accurate).

## Top Performers
1. **** - Delta-E 1.8
   - Near-OEM accuracy in blues/greens
   - Includes syringe with blunt needle for clean refills
   - $16.99 for 5 refills (vs $28 OEM)

2. **JetBest Eco-** - Delta-E 2.3
   - Excellent reds/yellows
   - Bottle design reduces spills
   - $49.99 bulk kit (50+ refills)

## Avoid These
- **** - Delta-E 6.9
  - Greens shifted teal, skin tones oversaturated
  - 37% of test prints showed banding

## Pro Tip
For critical color work, always:
1. Run a nozzle check post-refill
2. Print a calibration page
3. Store unused ink in amber glass bottles (light degrades dyes)
