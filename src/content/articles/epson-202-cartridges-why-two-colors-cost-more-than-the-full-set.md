## The Epson 202 Pricing Paradox

Epson’s 202 cartridge series (cyan, magenta, yellow, black) follows a common industry tactic: individual color cartridges often cost more per milliliter than multipacks. Here’s the breakdown for current Amazon pricing:

- **Single black (T202020)**: $16.99
- **Single color (T202120, T202220, T202320)**: $18.99 each
- **Multipack (all 4 cartridges)**: $49.99

Buying just cyan and magenta? That’ll run you $37.98 — $12.01 more than getting the full set with two extra cartridges you might not need immediately.

## Why This Happens

1. **Razor-and-blade model**: Printer manufacturers subsidize hardware costs by locking users into proprietary ink systems.
2. **Demand asymmetry**: Black ink is used more heavily, so its standalone price is often lower to avoid sticker shock.
3. **Bundle psychology**: Multipacks create perceived value while discouraging third-party alternatives.

## Smarter Alternatives

- **Third-party compatibles**: Reliable brands like InkOwl (B0G1R37VJC) offer full 4-packs for $16.99 — less than Epson’s single-color price.
- **EcoTank printers**: If you print frequently, models like the ET-2800 (B0FVY8TTVN) cut ink costs by 90% with refillable tanks.
- **Ink subscription services**: Epson’s ReadyPrint program (when available) can normalize costs for high-volume users.

Always check yield ratings (page counts) when comparing prices. Some third-party cartridges like the AIVITON 202 series (B0DVT2JTRN) offer 30% more pages per cartridge at half the OEM price.
