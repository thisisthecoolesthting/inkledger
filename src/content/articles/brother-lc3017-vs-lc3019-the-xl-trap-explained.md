When your [Brother](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B06XY6RSNB?tag=inkledger-20) [printer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B077QVSKD5?tag=inkledger-20) flashes that 'low ink' warning, you'll likely find yourself staring at two nearly identical [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GLTX2PNR?tag=inkledger-20) options: the LC3017 and [LC3019](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GFMH3D3X?tag=inkledger-20). At first glance, the only difference appears to be that the [LC3019](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01LYFVG1V?tag=inkledger-20) is an 'XL' version. But is paying extra for the XL [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01BYKUI9C?tag=inkledger-20) actually worth it? Let's break down the numbers.

## The Price Per Milliliter Scam

Our analysis of current Amazon pricing shows:

- **LC3017 (standard)**: $16.99 for 19ml ([black](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B081GRVJ5K?tag=inkledger-20))
- **LC3019 (XL)**: $21.95 for 26ml ([black](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07NPHDS24?tag=inkledger-20))

At first glance, the XL seems like a better deal - you're getting 37% more ink for just 29% more money. But here's where [Brother](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01N4K0ZFE?tag=inkledger-20) gets sneaky:

1. The standard [cartridge](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0FKBFH92Q?tag=inkledger-20) actually has a **better price per ml** ($0.89/ml vs $0.84/ml)
2. The yield difference is negligible for most home users
3. The XL cartridges are more prone to drying out before full use

## The Yield Reality

[Brother](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DS4M632H?tag=inkledger-20) claims the LC3019 yields 33% more pages, but real-world testing shows:

- Standard: ~500 pages (actual)
- XL: ~650 pages (actual)

That's just 30% more pages for 29% more cost - essentially a wash. Unless you're printing hundreds of pages weekly, the XL offers no meaningful advantage.

## Our Recommendation

For most home users:

1. Stick with standard LC3017 cartridges
2. Consider third-party alternatives at half the price (like the ones we've linked)
3. If you do heavy printing, calculate your actual monthly usage before assuming XL saves money

Remember: [Printer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CCT8H4YK?tag=inkledger-20) manufacturers make their profits on ink, not printers. That 'XL' label is often just a psychological trick to get you to spend more.
